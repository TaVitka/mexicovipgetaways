Weather, Culture and Safety

Weather and Climate

Discover Mexico's diverse climate, ranging from tropical in the south to desert and temperate in the central and northern regions. Generally, Mexico experiences two main seasons: a dry season from November to May and a rainy season from June to October, with temperatures varying depending on altitude and proximity to the coast.

For optimal travel experiences, consider visiting coastal areas during the dry season for sunny beach days, while inland regions may offer more pleasant temperatures for exploration during the cooler months.

Local Customs and Etiquette

Gain insights into Mexican culture, where family, respect, and hospitality are highly valued, and social interactions often revolve around food, music, and celebrations. Visitors should be mindful of personal space, avoid discussing sensitive topics such as politics or religion, and greet others with a warm handshake or embrace, depending on the familiarity of the relationship.

Health and Safety

Ensure a safe and healthy trip to Mexico by staying informed about potential risks and taking necessary precautions. Exercise caution in crowded areas, avoid displaying valuables, and be vigilant against petty theft and scams. Stay informed about local weather conditions and follow the advice of authorities in the event of natural disasters such as hurricanes or earthquakes.
